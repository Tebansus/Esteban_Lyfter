Animals

Brown Bear

The brown bear is a large mammal known for its intelligence and solitary nature. It has a remarkable adaptability that allows it to live in a wide variety of ecosystems in North America (only North America), taking advantage of all available natural resources. It's more dangerous to humans than black bears.

  • Weight: 90 to 480 kg
  • Length: 1.5 to 2.5 meters
  • Food Source: Omnivore: plants, fruits, insects, fish and mammals.
  • Life Expectancy: 25 to 30 years

Lion

The lion is a powerful carnivorous feline and the only one that lives in social groups called prides. It primarily inhabits the African savannas, where its role as top predator is essential to maintaining ecosystem balance by regulating populations of large herbivores.

  • Peso: 120 to 270 kg
  • Longitud: Up to 3.3 meters
  • Food Source: Carnivore: mainly large mammals such as zebras, wildebeasts, and buffalo.
  • Life Expectancy: 15 to 16 years

Shark

The great white shark is the world's largest predatory fish, a solitary and feared animal. To survive, it must constantly move, which allows it to allow oxygen into its body through its gills and avoid sinking, as it lacks a swim bladder.

  • Weight: Up to 2000 kg
  • Length: Up to 6.5 meters
  • Main food sources: Carnivore: fish and squid, marine mammals such as seals.
  • Life expectancy: 40 - 70 years
